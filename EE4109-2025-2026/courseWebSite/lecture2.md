# Lecture 2: Principle of Amplification and Biasing (13-11-2025)

## Overview
This lecture covers foundational concepts in amplifier design, focusing on how amplification works and biasing techniques for transistor circuits.

## Key Learning Resources

**Presentations:**
- "Principle of amplification" - demonstrates constructing basic amplifier stages using transresistance devices
- "Biasing implementation" - explains methods for implementing biasing in single-device amplifier stages
- "Quiz Biasing Techniques" - summarizes relevant biasing concepts

**Video Content:**
Several recorded lectures are available on YouTube covering principle of amplification, biasing implementation, and quiz result discussions.

## Study Materials

The curriculum references **Chapter 3** and **Chapter 9.4** for foundational knowledge, plus **Section 9: Introduction to Biasing** from the homologation program.

## Interactive Components

- **LDR Demo** - Light-dependent resistor demonstration of amplification principles
- **Biased NMOS Visualization** - Scrollable diagram illustrating operation of a biased NMOS stage
- **Group Exercise** - Hearing loop receive amplifier specifications (continuation from Lecture 1)

## Downloads

LTspice circuit examples demonstrating common-source stage biasing techniques are provided as supplementary materials.
