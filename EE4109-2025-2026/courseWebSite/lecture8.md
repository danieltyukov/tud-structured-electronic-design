# Lecture 8: Design of Application-Specific Multi-Stage Negative Feedback CMOS Amplifiers (04-12-2025)

**Time:** 08:45-10:30
**Location:** Hall Ampere

## Overview
This lecture covers designing multi-stage CMOS amplifiers using negative feedback techniques and balanced configurations discussed in prior sessions.

## Key Topics

**A Palette with Stages**
The lecture builds on four previously covered amplifier types: CS stages, balanced stages, feedback stages, and balanced feedback stages. The focus examines "how design techniques like balancing and negative feedback impact performance metrics including noise, drive capability, power efficiency, accuracy, bandwidth and weak nonlinearity."

**Design Considerations**

Three presentations are referenced:

1. "Controller Design" – addresses stage selection, relevant performance aspects, and design parameters for input/output stages
2. "Preferred Controller Stages" – presents two configurations: the inverting CS-CG cascode and noninverting CD-CG stage, noting they "show approximate unilateral behavior"
3. "Contribution of Stages to LP product" – analyzes how various stages contribute to loop gain-poles product and calculates minimum stage requirements for bandwidth specifications

**Additional Resources**
- "Pole-splitting and Cascode stages" presentation discusses Miller effect elimination through cascading
- "Interconnecting Controller Stages" presentation covers stage interconnection methods
- Study references: Chapters 5.3, 13.2.3, 14.2.6
