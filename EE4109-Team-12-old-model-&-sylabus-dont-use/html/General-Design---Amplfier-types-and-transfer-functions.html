General Design - Amplfier types and transfer functions

A1 amplifier type

A1 needs to be voltage to voltage, because the microphone is voltage and the induced electic field is a voltage from the coil.

At 1kHz the microphone coil equals -59.4 dBV/(A/m), the induced voltage for 1kHz equals

The transfer is an integrator with a certain voltage gain to match the output voltage of the microphone toto the output voltage of the coil. The integration is 1/s, and first we will convert the 70 dBSPL of the coil to voltage. 70dbSPL written as ... pascal which gets converted to voltage on the A2 input.

$V_{ind 1khz} = 0.1062\left[\mathrm{V}\right]$

Now we are going to calculate the magnatic signal to electic signal for the input of A1. We assume the magnatic field scales linearly with the dBSPL

\begin{equation} V_{ind 1khz}=0.09644 \end{equation} \begin{equation} V_{out}=V_{in} + V_{offset} \end{equation}

A2 amplifier type

A2 also needs to be a voltage amplfier to saturate the ADC for maximum resolution. The ADC is a voltage to digital converter.

For now this amplifier is not needed so let make it a wire
